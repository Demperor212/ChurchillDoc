Highschool

First thing first, high school is harder than junior high (which is kinda obivous, but still worth saying)

If you can just not study and score good in junior high, either because you are smart or because you learned ahead, congratulations! You can't do that in high school anymore

Its because the pace high school courses go at is a bit hard to "learn ahead" (if you have finished calculus already somehow, ignrore this, you can still not learn and score high)

Example: math 10-1 you learn trig, not so advanced functions and a bunch of other stuff; math 20-1 you learn harder trig, more advanced functions like quadratics and bunch of other stuff like sequences and series; math 30-1 you learn actually hard trig, actually hard functions and transformations; math 31 you learn calculus

If you are learning those at IB pace (aka, finish all this in 2 years), there is little possibility for you to learn ahead too much without it impacting other subjects of yours.

(Did I made it sound like there aren't much stuff to learn in math? If I did sorry I don't mean to, those concepts for you can't just be "i know it exists", they have to be mastered)
